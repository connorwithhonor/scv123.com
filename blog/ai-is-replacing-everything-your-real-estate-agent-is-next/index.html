The Week AI Ate the Service Industry

This week, three things happened that every homeowner in Santa Clarita should understand. Not because they are about real estate. Because they are about what services actually cost in 2026.

Perplexity, the AI search company, hit $450 million in annual recurring revenue. They got there by launching a tax agent that uploads your documents, matches them to IRS forms, and drafts your return. The cost? Seventeen dollars a month. The average American pays hundreds to a CPA or spends entire weekends fighting with TurboTax. A quarter of American workers plan to use AI for taxes this year. That number doubled in twelve months.

Anthropic launched something called managed agents. Eight cents per runtime hour plus model costs. If a business wants an AI agent that handles scheduling, invoices, customer inquiries, or data processing, they used to need months of engineering work and a team of developers. Now they need days and pocket change. Notion, Rakuten, and Asana are already shipping products on this platform.

Shopify dropped an AI toolkit that connects directly to Claude Code and Cursor. AI agents can now manage entire online stores. Bulk SEO updates. Product image swaps. Discount applications. One person with an AI agent does the work of an entire operations team. Shopify built the bridge and told everyone to walk across it.

Accountants. Receptionists. Marketing teams. Operations staff. All of them are being repriced by AI. Not eliminated. Repriced. The work still gets done. It just costs a fraction of what it used to.

Now ask yourself this question. If AI can do your taxes for $17 a month, if AI agents cost 8 cents an hour, if one person can run an entire store with AI, why does it still cost a percentage of your home's value to sell it?

What the Santa Clarita Market Looks Like Right Now

Median home value: $745,000. That is up 4% year over year. Five-year appreciation sits at 21.7%. A homeowner who purchased at $612,000 five years ago has gained roughly $133,000 in equity.

Supply: 2.1 months. Anything under 4 months favors sellers. Santa Clarita is firmly a seller's market. Inventory is low because owners locked into 2.5% and 3% rates during 2020 and 2021 cannot stomach a move into a 6.5% rate. Golden handcuffs. That keeps supply tight and prices firm.

Average days on market: 56. That is up from last year. Pricing matters more than ever. Overpriced homes sit. Correctly priced homes still move. The list-to-sale ratio is 97.4%, meaning sellers are getting within 3% of asking.

Mortgage rates: approximately 6.5%. If inflation fears stick through summer, we could see 7% to 7.5%. For sellers, this means buyers have less purchasing power. For buyers, it means less competition. For everyone, it means pricing right the first time is the difference between selling and sitting.

In a market where every dollar of equity counts, paying a percentage commission is a decision you make. It is not a requirement. It never was.
